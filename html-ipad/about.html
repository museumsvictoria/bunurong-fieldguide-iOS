About the
Bunurong App

This Field Guide app portrays the rich marine life found in Bunurong Marine National Park and nearby waters, in South Gippsland, Victoria, Australia. It presents imagery and descriptions of over 300 marine animal and plant species, with over half represented by images taken within the park boundaries. Over 700 marine species have been recorded from the park and this app presents those commonly encountered and others of special interest.

The Field Guide is a product of the Under the Lens program, a Parks Victoria and Museum Victoria collaboration that aims to showcase Victoria's rich marine life and raise the profile of marine protected areas across Victoria. The Under the Lens program now supports the Park Bioscans program, a long-term collaborative partnership between Parks Victoria and Museum Victoria.

For up-to-date information on Bunurong Marine National Park and other parks in Victoria, see the Parks Victoria website or call 13 19 63. For local information and educational and holiday programs, see the Bunurong Environment Centre website or call 03 5674 3738.

Acknowledgements

This project was jointly developed by Parks Victoria and Museum Victoria. Funding for this project was provided by Parks Victoria.

Parks Victoria is a statutory authority responsible for the day-to-day management of over four million hectares of terrestrial and marine parks across the state of Victoria in southern Australia. It manages the state's 30 Marine Protected Areas, encompassing around 12% of Victoria's marine waters. The Healthy Parks Healthy People philosophy is central to all Parks Victoria activities, with the authority playing a lead role in connecting people and communities with parks and nature.

Museum Victoria is Australia's largest public museum organisation. It is responsible for Victoria's state reference and research collections, documenting Victoria's natural and cultural history, and providing public education and engagement programs at its three venues (Melbourne Museum, Scienceworks and the Immigration Museum), as well as offsite and online.
